# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Primary users are transit advocates and agency staff who need to inspect stops quickly, compare what the feed says with what riders actually experience, and capture field notes without adding process overhead.

## Product Purpose
Poles maps GTFS stop data and turns it into a lightweight review surface for stop amenities. It exists to make field verification faster: drop in a stop dataset, pick a stop, see the feed metadata, record what is actually there, and build a practical stop-by-stop picture of shelter, seating, lighting, signage, and access.

## Brand Personality
Useful, quick and dirty, simple. The tone should feel direct, lightweight, and operational rather than polished for its own sake.

## Anti-references
This should not feel like a burden. Avoid bureaucratic forms, over-produced civic marketing pages, or enterprise dashboard chrome that makes a simple field note feel heavier than the task itself.

## Design Principles
1. Get to the stop fast: the map, search, and selected-stop state should stay immediate.
2. Keep the reporting cost low: every interaction should feel lighter than opening a spreadsheet or filing a ticket.
3. Let the tool disappear into the task: styling should support legibility and orientation, not compete with the map.
4. Show official data beside field truth: source metadata and crowd notes should complement each other, not merge into one ambiguous record.
5. Prefer practical clarity over polish theater: concise labels, obvious actions, and restrained surfaces win.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Default to strong text contrast, clear keyboard focus, and reduced visual noise. Motion should remain functional and minimal. The interface should be usable in bright daylight on mobile and on desktop by staff reviewing stop conditions for extended periods.
